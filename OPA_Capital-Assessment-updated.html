Capital Assessment

Please confirm what, if any, capital the client holds.

Property and Land

Such as their former matrimonial home

What if the property is in the name of the opponent?

Please do not answer yes should the property be held solely in the name of the opponent to the proceedings.

Bank Accounts, Shares, Investments and Savings

E.g. Current, Savings, Building Society, National Savings, Post Office or ISA.
Such as a dependent child.

How should I answer in regard to Inheritance & Trusts?

If some or all of the assets to be inherited are to be held on trust, then please answer yes to the question in relation to 'Trusts' and give the details accordingly. Please answer yes to the question in relation to being a beneficiary for the assets to be inherited that are not to be held on trust. If the client has an interest in a trust the provisional assessment will take this asset into account. Upon submission to the LAA further consideration will be given.

Other

What items are applicable?

Don’t include
  • Furniture and appliances
  • Your TV
  • Mobile phones, laptops and other devices
  • Clothing
  • The vehicle you use every day, unless it is a classic car or luxury vehicle
  • Equipment or tools you use for your work
Do include
  • Jewellery
  • Art
  • Antiques
  • Caravans and other vehicles you don’t use every day
  • Musical instruments, unless you’re a professional musician
Not as a result of a will or arising from the running of a business
This would include any cash at Home.

Assets Held Overseas

If the client and/or partner (if applicable) hold any of the above assets overseas you must answer yes to the relevant questions above.
i.e. 'USD1.7654' or 'EURO 0,8765'

Please note should you answer yes to any of these questions, especially bankruptcy, that the provisional assessment may be incorrect. Please consider the application carefully.
