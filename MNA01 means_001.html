Means Assessment

This interview is designed to collect information about the client so as to ascertain if he or she is:
1. Not subject to a means test owing to the proceedings applied for and the client’s status.
2. Is a child who meets the criteria for passporting.
3. Is in direct or indirect receipt of a passporting benefit and subject to the capital means test and therefore collect the details upon which the assessment will be based.*
4. Subject to the full means test and therefore collect the details upon which the assessment will be based.
5. Following the full or capital means test, eligible for Legal Aid. (Note: a provisional result will not always be possible).
If the client is subject to a means test (full or capital) the questions have been written to collect information that will assist a caseworker to determine financial eligibility.
The interview, where it is possible, will also provide a provisional result on eligibility and contributions.
At any point of the interview you can cancel your progress or choose to save progress to enable you to re-visit at a later stage.
Upon going through the means assessment the system will validate the answers given to ensure that they are in the format we require.
Upon completing and thereafter opting to submit the application to the LAA the system will re-run everything entered to do a final validation check.
* From 1 April 2013 those clients in receipt of a passporting benefit applying for new funding will be required to complete a capital means test.
Please note that at any point through the interview help text can be obtained by opening the text and scrolling down to the relevant section.
